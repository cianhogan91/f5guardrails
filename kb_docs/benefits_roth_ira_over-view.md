# Roth IRA — Quick Internal Explainer (Approved)

## What a Roth IRA is
A Roth IRA is an individual retirement account where contributions are made with after-tax dollars.

## Key characteristics
- Contributions are after-tax (you don’t deduct them from taxable income in most cases).
- Qualified withdrawals in retirement are typically tax-free (rules apply).
- Contribution limits and income eligibility may apply based on jurisdiction and regulation.

## Common comparison with 401(k)
- Roth IRA is individual and typically opened with a brokerage.
- 401(k) is employer-sponsored and often includes employer matching (if offered).
